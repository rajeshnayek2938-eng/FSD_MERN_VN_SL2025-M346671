Explore The Solar System

Our Solar System is a collection of celestial bodies orbiting the Sun, our central star, within the Milky Way Galaxy. It features eight planets: the inner rocky worlds (Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars) and the outer gas/ice giants (Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune). Moons, asteroids, comets, and dwarf planets also journey around the Sun, held by its immense gravitational pull. Jupiter, the largest, and Mercury, the smallest and closest, highlight planetary diversity. Earth stands out as the only known planet with life, supported by water and air. Planets rotate on their axes, causing days, and revolve around the Sun, causing seasons. The Sun itself contains nearly all the system's mass, providing light and heat. This cosmic neighborhood is a dynamic system of ongoing study and wonder

Solar System

Our solar system is located in the Milky Way, a barred spiral galaxy with two major arms, and two minor arms. Our Sun is in a small, partial arm of the Milky Way called the Orion Arm, or Orion Spur.

Sun

The Sun is the star at the heart of our solar system. Its gravity holds the solar system together, keeping everything — from the biggest planets to the smallest bits of debris — in its orbit.

Moon

Billions of years ago, the Moon was a molten world. As it solidified, the heaviest materials (metals like iron) sank down into the Moon’s center, and the lighter materials (lower density rocks) rose towards the surface.
